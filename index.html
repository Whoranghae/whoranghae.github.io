BubuDesuWho

BubuDesuWho is a rebuild of and tribute to the original GanbaWhoby by gacha4life — a game I kept coming back to long after its last update. This version extends it with more groups, more songs, higher-quality audio, and a pile of new features built on top.

BubuDesuWho tests your ability to recognize individual singers in μ's, Aqours, and Nijigasaki songs. New to Love Live? Start with the Love Live! Wiki.

How to play

  1. Pick a song from the menu on the left — sort by date or A–Z, filter by group, or search by title
  2. As the song plays, the time slots at the bottom highlight one at a time. Each slot is (usually) a single member, subunit, or year group
  3. Select who you think is singing
  4. When you're done guessing choosing, hit Check — or use the per-slot Check button to verify one line at a time
  5. Try as many times as you like until you get them all

Controls

Autoscroll follows the active slot as the song plays.

Themed toggles special per-song color themes.

The 🎨 palette button in the top-right swaps between the site's theme colors and the official member colors (Honoka orange, Chika orange, Ayumu pink, and so on).

The 🌙 / ☀ toggle next to it switches between dark and light mode. Both modes are theme-aware across slots and lyrics.

Reveal shows every answer — handy as a singer reference or when you just want to listen.

You can also pick a difficulty per song. Higher difficulties add slots that are not guaranteed to be a solo, subunit, or year group (any combination is fair game), though there's never a slot where the entire song lineup sings together. Hard and Insane assignments may not be fully accurate — nobody knows who sang what until the song is performed live.

Songs with rough, unsynced timings show a timing approx badge in the menu so you know which mappings aren't tightly aligned.

Keyboard shortcuts:

  • space: toggle play/pause
  • left: move backwards 2 seconds
  • right: move forwards 2 seconds
  • c: check answers
  • ctrl+a: toggle global reveal

Lyrics

Click the Lyrics button in the top-right to enable lyrics mode — the current line being sung appears in romaji, and for songs where they've been transcribed, the Japanese lyrics show alongside. When a solo line is answered correctly or revealed, the highlight is color-coded to that member. In full lyrics mode, the regular game controls are replaced by buttons including Download, which exports the lyrics as a karaoke-ready ASS file.

Bubudle

Bubudle is a daily Wordle-style mini-game. It plays a random lyric clip and asks you to guess the singer(s), revealing progressive hints (lyric snippet → clip difficulty → song difficulty → song theme) until you get it. Includes streak tracking, a subunit filter, volume and seek controls, and a Skip button when a clip has you stumped.

Contributing

Spot a bad timestamp, a wrong singer, or an off difficulty? Or maybe I'm just missing your favorite song? Open a GitHub issue — include the song title, what's wrong, and how you think it should be.

Two ways to gather what to report:

  • Flag things as you play from the Bubudle page — Bad Timestamp, Flag Difficulty, or Flag Singer. Flags save locally; hit Export to download a .txt summary to paste or attach to the issue.
  • Fix it in place — while playing, click Edit in the top-right to adjust slot timings, difficulty, and singer assignments, then Save Mapping to download the corrected config JSON. Attach that file to the issue.

Looking to add a song? Chances are I couldn't find a CCL (color-coded lyrics) sheet. If you have one, attach it to a GitHub issue and send it my way. Or if you're interested in making your own, use the Submit Lyrics page to build a color-coded lyrics file, and do the same.
